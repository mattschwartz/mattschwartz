Interests

Since my first computer science class in high school, I have been designing and working on simple games in my spare time. More recently, I have felt comfortable enough to work on much larger games, with artwork, sounds and animations.

Through UT's Game Development program, I have been able to work on small teams of 3-4 people to make a game together. Two of the featured projects here, After and ROgre, were made this way.

I enjoy game design since it allows me to incorporate a vast array of other skillsets into a single application: digital graphics, artificial intelligence, networking and performance. I tend to use my personal game project, StoneQuest, as a testing ground for honing these skills and the game has evolved massively over the past 3 years.

After is a 2D puzzle platformer, created in collaboration with four other students at UT through the Game Development Program.

The player plays as the apparent lone survivor of a post-apocalyptic world whose self-found purpose is to scour the city for clues to his identity and purpose as well as to discover the cause of the recent city's destruction.
